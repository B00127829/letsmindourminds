Samaritans

What the team at Samaritans do:

The Samaritans are a charity organization that has more than 20,000 volunteers who run their helplines. The helpline can be called day or night for free and is there to help anyone who is struggling to cope and who needs someone to listen to them. They provide an ear to listen with no pressure and no judgement. The Samaritans helpline is to prevent crisis and to support people in times of need. The Samaritans also work with people in prisons, schools, hospitals, and on the rail network too, they work with people who are going through a difficult time and also train others to do the same. Samaritan's vision is that fewer people die of suicide and that's why they work hard to reach more and more people every day and to make suicide prevention a priority. The Samaritan's helpline number receives around 10,000 calls a day for help. They are there for anyone who needs help.
"During 2020, more than 20,000 people volunteered their time for Samaritans. Over 16,200 trained listening volunteers responded to calls for help. Over 3,200 volunteers supported the running of our 201 branches. Over 1,000 prisoners volunteered as trained Listeners."
